Not just "best in tier" - these players are within-tier outliers (well above their tier's average) and their cross-tier skill score projects them into a higher tier. "Tier dominance" is how many standard deviations above their tier's average they sit; "Projects to" is the tier their skill would place them in if everyone were re-sorted by ability.

How this is estimated: cross-tier skill = within-tier dominance + a tier-strength bonus (Premier counts most). It's a model estimate - there are no cross-tier games to measure the exact gaps - so treat it as a strong signal, not gospel.
